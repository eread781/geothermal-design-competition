Data Visualization and Spatial Pattern Analysis of Geothermal Resources

With the creation of the Geothermal Data Repository, users were given the ability to freely submit and view all research funded by the U.S. Department of Energy. This project is focused around improving data visulization of the GDR resources as well as developing a better understanding of the spatial patterns of national research.

Application

The Challenge Question

How can geospatial mapping increase our understanding of geothermal energy? How can GIS improve how we visualize and communicate about geothermal energy?

Background Information

According to the Department of Energy’s strategic plan in May 2011, the DOE shifted its focus from merely completing projects and experiments to improving the dissemination of resources. This shift in focus demonstrates the DOE’s understanding of the immense value that comes with the availability of data. The open availability to researchers, students, investors, as well as the general public is the means to advancing the study of geothermal energy as well as promoting its future use as a renewable energy source. To accomplish this goal of data availability and dissemination, the Geothermal Data Repository was created to serve as the submission point, user interface, and storage source for all research funded by the DOE. The purpose of this project is to develop a GIS application that allows users to better visualize the GDR submissions as well as understand research spatial patterns on a national scale. The current GDR interface allows users to search based on types of geothermal energy or key words. However, the application we have developed displays the GDR metadata on a map. With the variety of other GIS tools included in the application, the application improves the efficiency of connecting a desired resource to any user. Ultimately, it is our goal that our Web GIS application serves as a means to advance the use of geothermal energy throughout the United States.

"Research is creating new knowledge." ~Neil Armstrong

Project Objectives

Understand the Value of Research

Research serves as the bedrock to advancing the use of geothermal energy. Access to geothermal resources and data enables better decision making, deeper understanding, as well as a source of advocacy for geothermal energy.

Improve Data Visualization

Rather than merely searching for resources based on key words, users will have the ability to view data submissions on a national scale. Thus, users will be able to better hone in on areas of interest as well as use various GIS tools to manipulate the data.

Analyze Spatial Patterns of Research

By creating a map-view of all geothermal research, users will be able to view national research on a wholistic scale. Researchers, investors, and the general public will be able to better visualize research patterns and trends.

Advance the Use of Geothermal Energy

Through accomplishing the previous three objectives, the overarching purpose of this project is to promote the advancement of geothermal energy in the United States.
